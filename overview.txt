COMPANY MANAGEMENT SYSTEM (CMS) — Overview

📦 GitHub: https://github.com/intsurjeetkaran-droid/cms

What is this system?
--------------------
A digital platform that helps a company manage its employees, work tasks,
attendance, salaries, and leave requests — all in one place.

There are three types of users in the system:

  ADMIN      — The company owner or HR head. Has full control over everything.
  MANAGER    — A team lead. Manages their team's tasks, attendance, and payroll.
  EMPLOYEE   — A regular staff member. Can view their own data and do daily tasks.

Each person logs in with their email and password and sees only what they are
allowed to see based on their role.


===========================================
HOW EACH ROLE WORKS
===========================================

ADMIN
-----
The admin is the most powerful user. They set up the entire system.

What admin can do:
  - Create departments (e.g. Engineering, Sales, HR)
  - Add managers and assign them to departments
  - Add employees to the system
  - View all employees, tasks, attendance, payroll, and leave requests
  - Approve or reject leave requests from both employees AND managers
  - Generate salary (payroll) for any employee
  - Bulk mark employees as absent for a specific date

What admin CANNOT do:
  - Apply for leave (admin is not an employee)
  - Create tasks (only managers do that)


MANAGER
-------
A manager leads a team. They are assigned to exactly ONE department.
Everything a manager does is limited to their own department only.
They cannot see or touch data from other departments.

What manager can do:
  - Add new employees — but ONLY to their own department
  - Create tasks and assign them — but ONLY to employees in their department
  - View their team's attendance — but ONLY their department's employees
  - Generate salary for team members — but ONLY their department's employees
  - View their team's payroll — but ONLY their department's employees
  - View and approve/reject leave requests — but ONLY from their department's employees
  - Apply for their own leave

What manager CANNOT do:
  - See employees, tasks, attendance, payroll, or leaves from other departments
  - Approve their own leave (must be approved by admin)
  - Approve another manager's leave
  - Delete employees
  - Create other managers
  - Do anything until admin assigns them to a department


EMPLOYEE
--------
A regular staff member who uses the system daily.

What employee can do:
  - View their own profile (name, department, salary, etc.)
  - Check in when they arrive at work
  - Check out when they leave
  - View their own attendance history
  - View tasks assigned to them
  - Mark a task as DONE — once done, it cannot be changed back
  - View their own salary slip for each month
  - Apply for leave
  - View the status of their leave requests

What employee CANNOT do:
  - See other employees' data
  - Create tasks
  - Generate payroll
  - Approve anyone's leave


===========================================
MODULES EXPLAINED
===========================================

1. DEPARTMENTS
--------------
The company is divided into departments (e.g. Engineering, Marketing, HR).
Each department has exactly one manager.

Rules:
  - A department can be created without a manager first
  - Only one manager per department — you cannot assign two managers to the same department
  - A manager can only belong to one department at a time
  - Only admin can create departments and assign managers
  - The correct setup order is:
      1. Admin creates departments
      2. Admin creates managers (no department needed at this step)
      3. Admin assigns each manager to a department
      4. Admin adds employees (department required)


2. EMPLOYEES
------------
Every person in the system (except admin) is an employee.
When an employee is added, they automatically get a login account.

Rules:
  - Minimum salary is $20,000
  - Manager can add employees but ONLY to their own department
  - Manager cannot add employees to other departments
  - Admin can add both employees and managers to any department
  - When admin adds a MANAGER, no department is needed at that point —
    admin assigns the department later from the Departments page
  - When admin adds an EMPLOYEE, a department must be selected
  - On the manager's team page, employees added by admin show an "Admin" badge
    so the manager knows who was added by admin vs themselves
  - Deleting an employee does not delete their login account
  - A manager must be assigned to a department before they can add anyone


3. TASKS
--------
Managers assign work tasks to employees.

Rules:
  - Each task is assigned to one employee only
  - One employee can have many tasks
  - Only managers can create tasks
  - Manager can ONLY assign tasks to employees in their own department
  - Manager cannot assign tasks to employees in other departments
  - Employees can mark a task as DONE — but CANNOT revert it back to pending
  - Once a task is marked done, it stays done permanently
  - Admin can see all tasks but cannot create them

Task status:
  PENDING   — work not started or in progress
  DONE      — work completed


4. ATTENDANCE
-------------
Employees record their daily presence by checking in and out.

Rules:
  - An employee can only check in once per day
  - You must check in before you can check out
  - You cannot check out twice in the same day
  - If you check in → marked as PRESENT (any time)
  - If you don't check in at all → marked as ABSENT
  - If your leave is approved → marked as LEAVE (not absent)

Attendance status:
  PRESENT   — checked in (any time)
  ABSENT    — did not come to work
  LEAVE     — on approved leave (no salary deduction)


5. PAYROLL (SALARY)
-------------------
Salary is calculated at the end of each month based on attendance.

How it works:
  - Base salary is the fixed monthly salary set when the employee was added
  - For every day marked ABSENT, $500 is deducted from the salary
  - Days marked as LEAVE are NOT deducted (approved leave is protected)
  - Final salary = Base salary − (Absent days × $500)

Example:
  Base salary: $30,000
  Absent days: 3
  Deduction:   3 × $500 = $1,500
  Net salary:  $30,000 − $1,500 = $28,500

Rules:
  - Payroll can only be generated once per employee per month
  - Admin can generate payroll for any employee
  - Manager can ONLY generate payroll for employees in their own department
  - Employee can only view their own salary — cannot generate it


6. LEAVE
--------
Employees and managers can request days off.

How it works:
  1. Employee or manager submits a leave request with start date, end date, and reason
  2. The request shows as PENDING
  3. A manager approves or rejects leave requests — but ONLY from their own department's employees
  4. Admin approves or rejects manager leave requests (and can also approve any employee leave)
  5. If approved, those days are automatically marked as LEAVE in attendance

Rules:
  - Maximum 5 leave days per month per person
  - You cannot apply for overlapping dates (if you already have a request for those days)
  - Admin cannot apply for leave
  - A manager cannot approve their own leave
  - A manager cannot approve another manager's leave
  - A manager cannot approve leaves from employees in other departments
  - Once approved or rejected, the decision cannot be changed

Leave status:
  PENDING   — waiting for approval
  APPROVED  — leave granted, attendance updated automatically
  REJECTED  — leave denied


===========================================
APPROVAL CHAIN
===========================================

Who approves what:

  Employee leave  →  Manager (own dept only) OR Admin can approve
  Manager leave   →  Admin ONLY can approve

  Nobody can approve their own leave.
  Manager cannot approve leaves from other departments.


===========================================
MANAGER DEPARTMENT SCOPE — IMPORTANT
===========================================

A manager's access is completely limited to their own department.
This means:

  - They can only SEE employees from their department
  - They can only ADD employees to their department
  - They can only ASSIGN tasks to their department's employees
  - They can only VIEW attendance of their department's employees
  - They can only GENERATE payroll for their department's employees
  - They can only APPROVE leaves from their department's employees

If a manager has no department assigned, they cannot do any of the above
until admin assigns them to a department.


===========================================
SALARY DEDUCTION SUMMARY
===========================================

  PRESENT  →  No deduction
  ABSENT   →  $500 deducted per day
  LEAVE    →  No deduction (protected)


===========================================
DAILY WORKFLOW EXAMPLE
===========================================

A typical day in the system:

  Morning:
    Employee opens the app → goes to Attendance → clicks "Check In"
    System records the time and marks them present

  During the day:
    Employee opens Tasks → sees their assigned work
    Completes a task → clicks "Mark as Done"

  End of day:
    Employee goes to Attendance → clicks "Check Out"
    System calculates how many hours they worked

  End of month:
    Manager goes to Payroll → selects employee + month → clicks "Generate Payroll"
    System counts absent days, calculates deduction, saves the salary record
    Employee can view their salary slip under Payroll


===========================================
LEAVE REQUEST WORKFLOW EXAMPLE
===========================================

  1. Employee goes to Leaves → fills in dates and reason → submits
  2. Manager sees the request under "Team Leave Requests"
  3. Manager clicks Approve or Reject
  4. Employee sees the updated status under their Leave History
  5. If approved, those days automatically show as LEAVE in attendance
     and will NOT be deducted from salary when payroll is generated
